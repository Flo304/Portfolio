What is Syn? Syn is about creating spaces for us to connect with non-human organisms in urban environments, using scientific knowledge and hands-on prototyping. Nature is captivating, yet it operates in ways that differ from how we think or how artificial systems work. Sometimes, its complexity can feel almost like *magic*. Let’s explore the micro world to better understand it from our human perspective.

During my Master's research, I became fascinated by non-human intelligences. Our current Anthropocene environment often makes us forget that humans aren’t the only beings with intelligence; other organisms, too, can solve problems beyond our comprehension. This realisation is humbling and challenges us to rethink the hierarchy of life. For example, micro-organisms play a vital role in our ecosystem. Though invisible to the naked eye, we depend on them more than we realise.

The installation consisted of a totem made out of wood, containing microbial fuell cells filled with soil, hooked to a power harvester board and Arduino. The installation investigated galvanic reactions in soil and microbial fuel cells as a way to expose scientific principles and render them accessible to other designers or artists. Galvanic reactions and microbial fuel cells are both ways to interact and prototype with energy produced by our local environment.

The soil microbial fuel cells are placed together to gather energy from each cell. The energy is then used to displace the body of soil on its own when human presence is detected. By using the combination of non-human intelligence, human and computational tools, we empower the body of soil to have a different hybrid interaction in our environment.
